    Historic Culture

    Yulin, known as Yulin in ancient times, has the reputation of being "a thousand-year old state and the capital of Lingnan". It has a history of more than 2,000 years, and has gradually developed a unique Lingnan culture, overseas Chinese culture, Hakka culture and jade merchant culture.

    The culture of Yulin is dominated by two cultures, the Guangfu culture and the Hakka culture, which are distinctive from each other, but show a tendency of mutual integration and assimilation. For those who belong to the Guangfu folk system, they tend to seek "wealth" and profit from trade and industry, while for those who belong to the Hakka folk system, they tend to seek "nobility" and respect literature and education, and cultivate and read the family. The main folk of Guangfu are in Yuzhou, Fumian, Beiliu, Rongxian and Xingye counties (cities and districts), while the Hakka folk are in Bobai and Luchuan.

    With a total area of 12,800 square kilometers, Yulin City has 7 counties (cities and districts) under the jurisdiction of Beiliu City, Rong County, Luchuan County, Bobai County, Xingye County, Yuzhou District, Fumian District and Yudong New District, with a total population of 7.41 million at the end of 2020, making it the second most populous city in Guangxi, with more than 2.2 million overseas Chinese and compatriots from Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan, making it the largest and nationally famous overseas Chinese town in Guangxi. Yulin is a national pilot city for reform and development, a national pilot zone for rural reform, one of the nine pilot zones for cross-strait agricultural cooperation, a member city of Guangxi Beibu Gulf Economic Zone and Pearl River-Xijiang Economic Zone. In 2020, the city achieved a gross regional product of 176.108 billion yuan, an increase of 3.3%. In the first half of this year, the city's gross regional product grew by 11.8%, with an average growth of 7.5% in two years; the value added of regular industry grew by 15.3%, fixed asset investment grew by 19.8%, total retail sales of consumer goods grew by 19.2%, and fiscal revenue grew by 6.3%.
